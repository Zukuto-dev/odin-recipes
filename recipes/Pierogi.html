Pierogi

Description

Pierogi are traditional Polish dumplings made of soft, unleavened dough filled with a variety of savory or sweet ingredients. Common savory fillings include potatoes, cheese, sauerkraut, or ground meat, while sweet versions may contain fruits or sweetened cheese. Pierogi are typically boiled, then fried in butter until golden brown, and are often served with sour cream or fried onions.

Ingredients

  • Flour
  • Water
  • Eggs
  • Mashed potatoes or cheese (for filling)
  • Sauerkraut or ground meat (optional fillings)
  • Onions
  • Butter
  • Sour cream (for serving)

Steps

  1. Make the Dough: Mix flour, water, and eggs to create a soft dough. Knead and let it rest for 30 minutes, then roll out thinly.
  2. Prepare the Filling and Shape the Pierogi: Prepare mashed potatoes, cheese, or other fillings. Cut the dough into circles, place filling in the center, fold over, and seal the edges.
  3. Cook the Pierogi: Boil the pierogi in salted water, then fry in butter until golden. Serve with sour cream or fried onions.
