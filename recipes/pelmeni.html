Pelmeni

Description

Pelmeni are traditional Russian dumplings made of thin, unleavened dough filled with a variety of savory ingredients. The dough is typically made from flour, water, and eggs, creating a soft and pliable texture that is easy to work with. The fillings for pelmeni can vary, but the most common combinations include ground meat, such as beef, pork, or lamb, often mixed with onions, garlic, and spices like salt and pepper. Once filled, the dumplings are folded into small, crescent or round shapes, and pinched tightly to seal the edges. Pelmeni are usually boiled until tender and are served hot, often garnished with sour cream, butter, or vinegar.

Ingredients

  • Flour
  • Water
  • Eggs
  • Ground beef, pork, or lamb (for filling)
  • Onions
  • Garlic
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Sour cream (for serving)
  • Butter (for serving)

Steps

  1. Make the Dough: Mix flour, water, and eggs to form a smooth, elastic dough. Knead it briefly, then let it rest for about 30 minutes. Roll the dough out thinly on a floured surface.
  2. Prepare the Filling and Shape the Pelmeni: Combine ground meat (such as beef, pork, or lamb) with finely chopped onions, garlic, salt, and pepper. Cut the dough into small circles, place a spoonful of filling in the center of each, then fold the dough over the filling and pinch the edges to seal.
  3. Cook the Pelmeni: Boil the pelmeni in salted water for about 4-5 minutes until they float to the surface. Serve hot with sour cream, butter, or vinegar.
